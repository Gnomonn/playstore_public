Blurt — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026

Blurt ("we", "the app") is an anonymous secret-sharing app. This policy explains what data the app handles and why. By using Blurt you agree to this policy.

Anonymous by design

Blurt has no sign-up, no username, no email login. When you first open the app we create an anonymous account tied to a random ID. We cannot tell who you are, and secrets you post are not linked to any real-world identity.

Data we collect

  • Anonymous account ID — a random identifier used to keep your token balance and let you manage the secrets you posted. Not linked to your name, email, or device owner.
  • Secrets you write — the text you post is stored on our server and shown anonymously to other users. Do not include personal information in a secret; content you post is your responsibility.
  • App activity — basic actions such as posting, drawing, and reacting, needed for the app to function (e.g. token accounting).
  • Advertising data — our ad provider (Google AdMob) may collect a device advertising ID (AAID), approximate location (from IP), and diagnostic data to serve and measure ads.

Data we do NOT collect

  • Your name, email address, or phone number.
  • Your contacts, photos, files, or precise GPS location.
  • Any information that identifies you personally.

Advertising

Blurt shows banner ads via Google AdMob. AdMob may use advertising and device identifiers to serve personalized or non-personalized ads and to measure performance. Where required (e.g. in the EEA/UK), we ask for your consent before serving personalized ads, and you can change this choice at any time. See Google's policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Third-party services

  • Supabase — hosts the anonymous account and secrets data (backend database & anonymous authentication).
  • Google AdMob — serves ads (see above).

Data retention & deletion

Secrets expire automatically (typically within 24 hours, longer if others engage with them). You can delete your own secrets in the app at any time. Because accounts are anonymous, we cannot look you up by name — to request deletion of data associated with your anonymous ID, contact us at the email below and we will assist where technically possible.

Children

Blurt is not directed to children under 13 and may contain mature user-generated content. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
